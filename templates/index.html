OUR MODELS

Aromatase

Aromatase is an enzyme that plays a key role in the production of estrogen, a hormone that is known to promote the growth and proliferation of breast cancer cells. Aromatase inhibitors are a class of drugs that are used to treat hormone receptor-positive breast cancer, which accounts for about 70% of all breast cancer cases.

ALK

Anaplastic lymphoma kinase. ALK was originally identified as a gene that is fused with the NPM1 gene in anaplastic large cell lymphoma (ALCL), a type of non-Hodgkin lymphoma. This fusion protein, known as NPM-ALK, has constitutive kinase activity and promotes cell proliferation and survival.

mTOR

mammalian Target Of Rapamycin, is a protein kinase that is involved in several cellular processes, including cell growth, proliferation, metabolism, and autophagy. It functions as a central regulator of cell signaling pathways and is activated by a variety of stimuli, such as growth factors, nutrients, and stress.

PI3K

Phosphoinositide 3-kinase, is a protein kinase that is involved in several cellular processes, including cell growth, proliferation, survival, and metabolism. It functions as a signaling molecule and is activated by a variety of stimuli, such as growth factors, hormones, and nutrients.

advantages of drug discovery using AI ML

Increased efficiency

AI/ML algorithms can process and analyze vast amounts of data quickly and accurately, which can accelerate the drug discovery process. This allows researchers to identify promising compounds more efficiently and focus their efforts on the most promising leads.

Reduced costs

Traditional drug discovery is an expensive and time-consuming process. By using AI/ML, researchers can reduce the cost of drug development by reducing the number of compounds that need to be synthesized and tested in the lab.

Improved accuracy

AI/ML algorithms can analyze complex datasets and identify patterns that may not be apparent to human researchers. This can lead to more accurate predictions of a compound's efficacy and safety, reducing the risk of failure in later stages of development.

Increased success rate

By identifying the most promising compounds earlier in the drug discovery process, AI/ML can increase the success rates of drug development. This can lead to more effective treatments for a variety of diseases, including those that are currently difficult to treat.
